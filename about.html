About

Researcher in psychology, numerical cognition, and neurocognitive methods.

I recently completed my PhD in Psychology at the University of Nottingham Malaysia, where my thesis examined the socio-neurocognitive mechanisms of single-digit multiplication fluency using behavioural, brain imaging, and cross-sectional approaches.

Biography

My work sits at the intersection of cognitive psychology, educational psychology, and neuroscience. I study how arithmetic fluency develops and varies across people, with particular interest in the roles of short-term memory, language-of-learning, academic background, and neural structure.

Across my doctoral training, I designed and ran one large sociodemographic study, three behavioural experiments, and one fMRI study, with data from more than 1,500 Malaysian and British university students. I have also contributed to EEG data collection, manuscript development, and international conference presentations.

Teaching Philosophy

My teaching emphasizes active engagement with empirical tools and critical thinking in psychological science. I aim to help students build technical confidence through practical, scaffolded instruction in statistics, experimental programming, and research methods, while adapting pacing and support to diverse backgrounds and learning styles.
